explore the Natural Beauty of Kashmir

Azad Kashmir, also known as Azad Jammu and Kashmir (AJK), is a self-governing region under Pakistani administration, covering an area of approximately 4,200 square miles. Located in the northwestern part of the Indian subcontinent, Azad Kashmir shares borders with the Pakistani provinces of Punjab and Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, as well as the Indian-administered territory of Jammu and Kashmir. The region is divided into three main areas: the Mirpur District, the Poonch District, and the Muzaffarabad District, with Muzaffarabad serving as the capital city. Azad Kashmir is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, with lush green valleys, snow-capped mountains, and picturesque lakes. The region has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of Kashmiri, Punjabi, and Pakistani influences, and is home to several ancient forts, temples, and mosques. With its stunning landscapes, warm hospitality, and strategic location, Azad Kashmir is a treasure trove of natural and cultural wonders.

Kashmir, often referred to as 'Paradise on Earth', is a majestic region nestled in the Himalayan mountains. With its unparalleled natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality, Kashmir has been a source of fascination for centuries. From the snow-capped peaks of the Pir Panjal range to the serene waters of Dal Lake, and from the vibrant Mughal gardens to the ancient temples and mosques, Kashmir is a treasure trove of breathtaking landscapes, diverse traditions, and warm hearts. Located in the northernmost part of India, Kashmir is a land of enchanting splendor, waiting to be explored and experienced
